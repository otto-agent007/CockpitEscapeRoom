# Working historical context

This file records context supplied by the project owner. It is useful for realism, scene dressing, route selection, and tone. It is not automatically verified publication copy.

## Northwest, Memphis, and the DC-9

The design should evoke the DC-9 as a rugged, paid-off, high-frequency short-haul workhorse feeding passengers into Memphis from smaller Southern and Midwestern communities. Larger Airbus and Boeing aircraft handled many longer or higher-density routes, while DC-9s supported the regional funnel.

Suggested environmental details:

- MEM as the center of a dense route-card network.
- Mechanical departure-board sounds and tightly grouped arrival/departure waves.
- Concourse B atmosphere.
- Route strips using LIT, JAN, BHM, BTR, SHV, MOB, STL, BNA, SDF, and IND.
- Northwest-era silver/red visual cues used as inspiration without copying a protected livery or logo.
- Later Delta-era details as optional archival texture.
- The emotional connection between the decline of the Memphis hub and the DC-9 retirement era.
- The “full-circle” idea that Delta was associated with both the beginning and end of major U.S. DC-9 service.

## Design rule

Use this history to make the cockpit and world feel grounded. Do not turn Captain Mode into an examination on fleet totals, merger dates, departure counts, retirement chronology, or aircraft economics.

Before final public release, independently verify any statement presented as fact. Keep a citation record in the asset/content manifest. Unverified details can still inspire fictional dispatch boards and atmosphere, but they should not be represented as documentary claims.
