# DC-9-51 Reference Report

## Target Cockpit

Primary target: Northwest-style McDonnell Douglas DC-9-51 cockpit, with N775NC as the seed aircraft where available. The reference hierarchy prioritizes real DC-9-51 Northwest cockpit material, then lower DC-9 variants only for shared analog layout, materials, or component families.

## Seed Sources

- `dc9_51_n775nc_cockpit_primary`: Wikimedia Commons cockpit photo of Northwest DC-9-51 N775NC. Highest-confidence seed for main panel silhouette, analog instrument density, yokes, glare shield, windshield framing, and blue-gray material family.
- `dc9_50_n775nc_exterior_context`: Wikimedia Commons exterior photo of the same aircraft family and registration context. It is not cockpit geometry authority.
- `dc9_40_northwest_cockpit_secondary`: Wikimedia Commons Northwest DC-9-40 cockpit. Useful for shared analog panel and material clues, but not a -51 source.
- `md81_midwest_cockpit_secondary`: Wikimedia Commons MD-81 cockpit. Lower-ranked DC-9-family derivative for generic analog density/material continuity only.
- `simulation_daily_dc9_release_presentation`: Simulation Daily article about a DC-9 simulator package. Presentation benchmark only; not target variant geometry.

## Missing Required Views

- Overhead panel close-up for Northwest DC-9-51.
- Pedestal/throttle quadrant close-up for Northwest DC-9-51.
- Captain sidewall and window mechanism details.
- First officer side view.
- Label and placard close-ups with confirmed redistribution rights.

## Modeling Notes

The single primary cockpit photo can establish the captain-eye main-panel read, but it cannot prove overhead/pedestal geometry or sidewall details. Before production cockpit modeling, collect or commission additional real-aircraft references and update the manifest with license and compatibility notes.
