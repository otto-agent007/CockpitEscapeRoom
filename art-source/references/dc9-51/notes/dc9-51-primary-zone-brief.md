# DC-9-51 Primary Zone Modeling Brief

Reference ID: `dc9_51_n775nc_cockpit_primary`

This brief translates the primary DC-9-51 cockpit photo into modeling zones for CockpitEscapeRoom. It is not a final geometry approval and does not replace variant-specific component evidence.

## Priority zones

### 1. Captain-seat cockpit silhouette

Use the primary image to establish the wide, low, analog DC-9 cockpit feel:

- broad blue-green/gray panel shell;
- dense round-gauge center field;
- black yokes with rectangular checklist placards;
- high glare shield with autopilot/annunciator band;
- throttle pedestal projecting forward from the lower center;
- overhead panel visible as a dense dark band above the windshield.

### 2. Main panel

Model as a layered metal instrument wall, not a flat image plane.

Required blockout features:

- left captain instrument cluster;
- right first-officer instrument cluster;
- center engine/system gauge stack;
- rectangular radio/autopilot control modules across the upper panel;
- colored annunciator lights and square pushbutton clusters;
- blue-green paint with localized wear and fasteners.

Do not attempt to read or reproduce operational labels from the photo yet. Use generic fictional labels until close-up sources are approved.

### 3. Yokes and columns

The yokes are high-priority hero objects.

Modeling notes:

- black double-grip yokes with rounded rectangular upper handle;
- central rectangular checklist/placard pad;
- vertical black columns down to the floor;
- symmetrical captain and first-officer placement;
- preserve separate pivotable column/yoke hierarchy for future interaction.

Use DC-9-32 source geometry only as a candidate if the source report records the variant mismatch and downstream repair needs.

### 4. Throttle pedestal

Use the primary image to block approximate scale and placement only.

Required blockout features:

- raised black pedestal in the lower center;
- paired throttle levers and adjacent levers;
- lower radio/navigation panels with knobs and numeric displays;
- worn top surfaces and metal edge highlights.

Need more close-up sources before production geometry.

### 5. Overhead panel

Use the primary image only for density and location.

Required blockout features:

- dark overhead panel strip above windshield;
- many toggle/knob shapes;
- small illuminated rectangles;
- strong perspective compression.

Do not finalize switch layout without additional overhead close-up sources.

### 6. Sidewalls and windshield

Use the primary image for framing and color only.

Required blockout features:

- sloped side window frames;
- dark windshield wipers and armatures;
- blue-green sidewall surfaces;
- daylight through forward glass;
- lower sidewall/floor clutter kept restrained.

Need left/right sidewall reference before final production mesh.
